Project Summary

Deaths from drug overdose continue to contribute to mortality in the United States. In the past 3 decades, drug overdoses have killed approximately 870,000 people. Since first wave of the opioid epidemic began in the 1990s, the epidemic has expanded to include other synthetic, illicit drugs, including Heroin, Cocaine, Meth, prescription opioids and psychostimulants.

(DISCLAIMER : Due to Heroku data limits, psychostimulants were removed from the dataset)

The quality of data reported by medical examiners varies by state. The CDC has noted that some states have a tendency to report drug poisoning deaths without listing a specific drug. The analysis incorporates this research to identify states that have higher death rates as a result of overdose in the United States from 2016-2019.
